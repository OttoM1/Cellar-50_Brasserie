Tasting Notes

Cabernet Sauvignon

The King of Reds. Full-bodied, high tannins, blackcurrant, green pepper.

Merlot

The Charmer. Softer than Cab. Plum, chocolate, velvet texture.

Pinot Noir

The Poet. Light-bodied, pale color. Strawberry, earth, mushrooms. Delicate.

Syrah / Shiraz

The Wild One. Full-bodied. Black pepper, meat, dark fruit, spice.

Chardonnay

The Chameleon.
Oaked: Butter, vanilla, toast.
Unoaked: Apple, lemon, crisp.

Sauvignon Blanc

The Zest. High acidity. Gooseberry, grass, lime, passionfruit.

Riesling

The Pure. Aromatic. Lime, green apple, petrol (aged), honey.

Young Wines (1-4 Years)

Reds: Vibrant color (purple/ruby). Fresh fruit flavors. Tannins feel "grippy" or drying.

Whites: Pale, greenish hints. Crisp acidity, fresh flowers and citrus.

Mature Wines (5-10+ Years)

Reds: Color fades to brick/orange. Fresh fruit turns to dried fruit (fig, prune). Flavors of leather, tobacco, forest floor emerge. Tannins soften.

Whites: Color deepens to gold/amber. Nutty, honeyed, savory notes replace fresh fruit.

France 🇫🇷

Bordeaux: Blends (Cab/Merlot). Earthy, structured.

Burgundy: Pinot Noir / Chardonnay. Elegant, terroir-driven.

Loire: Sauvignon Blanc (Sancerre). Mineral, sharp.

Italy 🇮🇹

Tuscany: Sangiovese (Chianti). Cherry, herbs, high acid.

Piedmont: Nebbiolo (Barolo). Tar, roses, high tannin.

USA 🇺🇸

Napa Valley: Cabernet & Chardonnay. Ripe, bold, oaky, high alcohol.

Oregon: Pinot Noir. Earthy, fruity, similar to Burgundy.

New Zealand 🇳🇿

Marlborough: Sauvignon Blanc. Intense tropical fruit, zesty.

Spain 🇪🇸

Rioja: Tempranillo. Vanilla, dill (from oak), red berry.
